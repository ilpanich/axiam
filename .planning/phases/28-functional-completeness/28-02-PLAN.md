<objective>
Give service-account (cert-auth / device-auth) tokens a self-describing subject kind (FUNC-04): add a
`SubjectKind` enum and a `sub_kind` claim to `AccessTokenClaims`, mint `ServiceAccount` via a dedicated
`issue_service_account_token` at the one SA call-site (resolving `TODO(T15)`), and stamp `User` /
`OAuth2Client` on the existing user and client-credentials mint paths — without touching `issue_access_token`'s
49 call sites. The claim is informational only (D-10): validators accept tokens regardless of `sub_kind`, and
tokens minted before this change deserialize as `User` (D-11). Also verify (no code) that admin user-listing
and admin MFA management are already RBAC-gated.

Purpose: SDK modeling and audit attribution can distinguish subject kinds. No authz behavior changes.
Output: Tokens self-describe their subject; existing tokens keep working through their TTL.
</objective>

<assumption_delta_decision>
Detector fired (pluralization "another"; chosen "custom") on this phase. The genuine architectural
signal is the **subject-kind pluralization** in FUNC-04: the token subject was previously a single
implicit kind (a `sub` string with no kind), and this plan introduces a plural `SubjectKind`
(User / ServiceAccount / OAuth2Client).

- Now-primary noun: **subject kind** of an access token.
- Decision: **add-alongside** — `User` remains the default/primary via `#[serde(default)]`; `ServiceAccount`
  and `OAuth2Client` are added alongside it. This is exactly what D-09/D-10/D-11 already lock; this block
  references those decisions rather than re-litigating them.
- Accepted debt: `sub_kind` is informational-only (D-10) — no validator or authz path reads it, so an
  attacker cannot use it to infer or gain privilege, and a mislabelled kind cannot change an authz outcome.
- What would force a later promote (informational → load-bearing): a future phase that gates endpoints by
  subject kind (e.g., rejecting `ServiceAccount` tokens on interactive routes). That is explicitly a
  Deferred Idea in CONTEXT.md and out of scope here.
- Invariant worth a test: "validators accept a token regardless of its `sub_kind`" (covered by the D-11
  backward-compat test plus a positive test that a `ServiceAccount` token validates like any other).

The other two detector signals ("another user's MFA", "custom template") are ordinary feature vocabulary,
not modeling-monopoly changes — no-change.
</assumption_delta_decision>
